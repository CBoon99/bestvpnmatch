Getting ChatGPT in China: what actually works

China's Great Firewall (GFW) blocks ChatGPT at multiple layers simultaneously — DNS resolution is poisoned, IP ranges are blacklisted, and deep packet inspection (DPI) actively identifies and drops VPN traffic that uses standard protocols. A regular VPN that works fine in the UK or US will typically fail in China within hours or days of a crackdown period.

What the GFW cannot easily defeat is obfuscated traffic — VPN connections disguised to look identical to ordinary HTTPS web traffic. This is the technology that separates the VPNs that work in China from the ones that don't:

  • ExpressVPN — Lightway protocol in obfuscated mode. Activates automatically when the app detects a restrictive network environment. This is the technology reason ExpressVPN leads this category.
  • NordVPN — Dedicated obfuscated servers, selectable manually in the app. Connect to one of the obfuscated server pool before attempting to access ChatGPT or Claude.
  • Astrill VPN — StealthVPN and OpenWeb protocols. Purpose-built for the GFW. Particularly well regarded among long-term China expats who have tried multiple alternatives.

Standard OpenVPN, WireGuard (without obfuscation), and L2TP/IPSec all fail in China under active inspection. Do not rely on them.

⚠️ Critical: Install before you travel

This cannot be overstated. The Apple App Store and Google Play Store in China do not carry VPN apps — they were removed under pressure from Chinese authorities. If you arrive in China without a VPN already installed and configured, you have almost no practical way to get one from inside the country.

Download and fully configure your VPN (including testing it once on your home network) before you board the plane. ExpressVPN and NordVPN both allow you to log in and configure the app before you need to use it. Keep your login credentials noted somewhere offline in case you need to reinstall.

Bypassing school and corporate network blocks

Outside of authoritarian internet jurisdictions, the most common reason people can't access ChatGPT or Claude is their own employer or institution. Corporate IT departments block AI tools for two distinct reasons: data security (concern that employees paste sensitive company information into AI tools) and productivity policy (blanket "social and AI" category blocking). University networks frequently mirror corporate policies.

Content filters like Zscaler, Cisco Umbrella, and Palo Alto Prisma Access work by inspecting DNS requests and URL categories. They do not break VPN connections. When you connect to a VPN before opening ChatGPT or Claude, your DNS request goes to the VPN's resolver, not your employer's, and your traffic exits through the VPN server's IP address. The content filter sees an encrypted tunnel and cannot categorise the destination.

What to use:

  • NordVPN obfuscated servers — most corporate firewalls pass obfuscated VPN traffic because it's indistinguishable from standard HTTPS business traffic. First choice for corporate environments.
  • ExpressVPN Lightway — equally effective on corporate firewalls. The lightweight protocol adds minimal latency, keeping the AI tools responsive.
  • Proton VPN — Stealth protocol is effective on restrictive networks and Proton's privacy credibility is strong for users with heightened security concerns.

Note: using a personal VPN on a corporate device may violate your employment agreement or acceptable use policy. Use a personal device if in doubt. We make no legal recommendations — this is technical information only.

Frequently asked questions

Real questions from people trying to access AI tools while abroad or on restricted networks.

Is ChatGPT blocked in China?

Yes, definitively. ChatGPT has been blocked by China's Great Firewall since shortly after its public launch in late 2022. OpenAI's entire infrastructure — chat.openai.com, the API endpoints, and the mobile apps — is blocked at both the DNS and IP level. China has also not approved ChatGPT for domestic distribution, which is why it was never available through the Chinese App Store.

By mid-2026, OpenAI has no presence in the Chinese market. There is no legal path for Chinese residents to access ChatGPT without a VPN. Foreign nationals visiting China are in a legal grey zone — VPNs are restricted but not specifically prohibited for foreigners, and enforcement against tourists and business travellers is rare.

Which VPN works best for ChatGPT in China?

Based on our Q2 2026 testing, ExpressVPN is the most consistently reliable option. Its Lightway protocol automatically activates obfuscation when it detects a restrictive network, and it maintains a large pool of servers optimised for GFW bypass.

Astrill VPN is a strong specialist alternative — its StealthVPN protocol is purpose-built for the GFW and has a strong track record during crackdown periods. It's the most recommended option in China expat communities in Beijing and Shanghai. NordVPN works with its obfuscated server setting enabled, and offers the best price-to-reliability ratio.

Free VPNs do not work reliably in China and we do not recommend them for this use case.

Can I use a free VPN to access ChatGPT?

Not reliably, and particularly not in countries with active censorship like China, Russia, or Iran. Free VPNs have fundamental limitations that make them unsuitable for this use case:

No obfuscation: The advanced protocol features required to bypass deep packet inspection are only available in paid products. Free VPNs use standard OpenVPN or IKEv2 which are trivially detected and blocked.

Bandwidth caps: Most free tiers limit you to 500MB–2GB per month. A normal AI tool session consumes data quickly, especially with image generation tools.

Data practices: Several prominent free VPNs have been caught logging and selling user data. If you're using AI tools for work or sensitive research, this is an unacceptable risk. Pay $3–7/month for a product that has been independently audited.

Does my company VPN block AI tools?

Your corporate VPN itself doesn't block AI tools — but your company's internet traffic likely routes through a content filtering proxy even when you're on the VPN. Products like Zscaler Internet Access, Cisco Umbrella, and Palo Alto Prisma classify AI tools as a category and apply blocking policy to that category.

If you can reach chat.openai.com from your home network but not from the office or while on company VPN, the block is being applied by a content filter, not your ISP. A personal VPN (used on a personal device) will bypass this filtering because your traffic exits through the VPN's server before it reaches any corporate filtering infrastructure.

Check your employment contract and acceptable use policy before doing this on a company device. On a personal device connected to your own data plan, there are no such restrictions.

Is it legal to use a VPN to access ChatGPT in restricted countries?

Legality varies significantly and this is not legal advice. Here is what we know about the jurisdictions most relevant to AI tool access:

China: VPNs require government approval. Unapproved VPNs (which includes all consumer VPNs) are technically illegal. Enforcement against individuals, particularly foreign nationals, is rare — but not impossible. Using a VPN in China is a personal risk calculation.

Russia: VPNs that comply with Roskomnadzor's blocking orders are legal. VPNs that help users access blocked content are prohibited. Enforcement against individuals has increased since 2022 but remains inconsistent.

Iran: Only government-approved VPNs are legal. The government itself uses VPNs to access blocked services, which is illustrative of how enforcement is applied.

Most other countries including EU, USA, UK, Canada, Australia: Using a VPN is completely legal, unrestricted, and common practice.
