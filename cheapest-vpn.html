Cheap VPNs to Avoid — and Why

Not all low-cost or free VPNs are created equal. Some are actively harmful to privacy.

!

Hola VPN (Peer-to-peer routing)

Hola VPN is not a traditional VPN — it routes your traffic through other users' devices and sells their bandwidth in a botnet-style network. This means a stranger's criminal activity could be attributed to your IP address, and vice versa. Multiple security researchers have documented this behaviour. Do not use Hola VPN under any circumstances.

!

TouchVPN and Betternet (Ad-supported data collection)

Both TouchVPN and Betternet are ad-supported VPNs with documented records of collecting and sharing user data with advertising networks. Security researchers have identified fingerprinting SDKs in their Android apps. The free tier is funded by building an advertising profile of your behaviour — the exact opposite of what a VPN should do.

?

VPNs with No Privacy Policy or Anonymous Ownership

Any VPN registered in Panama, Seychelles, or the British Virgin Islands with anonymous ownership and no verifiable team should be treated with extreme caution. These jurisdictions are popular for two reasons: legitimate privacy protection, and as a place to operate without legal accountability. Without knowing who runs a VPN, you can't evaluate whether their no-logs claim is credible. NordVPN (Lithuania-registered, Nord Security team publicly known) and Proton VPN (Swiss AG, public team) are the gold standard for corporate transparency.
