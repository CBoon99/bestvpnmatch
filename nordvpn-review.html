How NordLynx addresses WireGuard's privacy limitation

Standard WireGuard has a known privacy trade-off: it stores IP addresses in memory while a session is active. NordVPN's NordLynx wrapper adds a double NAT system that prevents IP correlation without requiring persistent storage. This gives NordLynx the speed advantages of WireGuard while maintaining the privacy posture needed for a no-logs service.

Extra anonymity layers

For users with elevated privacy needs, NordVPN offers Double VPN (two-hop routing through separate servers in different countries) and Onion over VPN (routes traffic through the Tor network via a dedicated server). Both features add latency but provide meaningful additional anonymity beyond standard VPN usage.

Important context — 2018 server breach: In 2018, one of NordVPN's rented servers in Finland was accessed by an unauthorised party who exploited an insecure remote management system left open by the hosting provider — not by NordVPN's own code. A forensic review confirmed the server contained no user data: no logs, no credentials, no browsing history. This was consistent with the no-logs policy working as claimed. NordVPN terminated the hosting arrangement, disclosed the breach publicly (some months later — a transparency criticism that is fair), and has since completed two Deloitte audits confirming no log storage. We include this because it matters — and because the outcome actually validates the no-logs architecture rather than contradicting it.

Is NordVPN safe?

Yes. NordVPN is independently audited by Deloitte (2022 and 2023), uses AES-256 encryption with the NordLynx (WireGuard-based) protocol, operates RAM-only servers that cannot store data persistently, and is headquartered in Panama — a jurisdiction outside the 5/9/14 Eyes surveillance alliances with no mandatory data retention laws.

A 2018 server compromise in Finland found no user data exposed — the no-logs architecture worked as claimed. Since then, NordVPN has completed two Deloitte audits, updated its infrastructure, and published a warrant canary updated quarterly (no national security letters received as of June 2026). We score NordVPN 94/100 for privacy and 92/100 for security.

Does NordVPN keep logs?

No. NordVPN operates a verified no-logs policy, independently confirmed by Deloitte in 2022 and 2023. Auditors confirmed that NordVPN stores no user activity logs, no connection timestamps, no IP addresses, and no traffic data during or after a session.

The Panama jurisdiction means no government can compel data retention. RAM-only servers mean that even in the event of physical server seizure, no data persists beyond the current power cycle. The 2018 breach of a Finnish server validated this in practice — investigators found nothing stored.

Does NordVPN work with Netflix?

Yes, consistently. per June 2026 editorial review, NordVPN successfully unblocked Netflix in all 6 regions tested: US, UK, Japan, Germany, France, and Canada. Streams loaded at 4K quality with no buffering on local servers.

NordVPN's SmartPlay technology automatically routes streaming traffic through the best-optimised servers for each platform — you simply connect to the relevant country server without any manual configuration. We score NordVPN 95/100 for streaming — second only to ExpressVPN at 98/100. It also successfully unblocked BBC iPlayer, Disney+, Hulu, Amazon Prime Video, and HBO Max based on published benchmarks.

Is NordVPN worth it in 2026?

Yes, for most users. At $3.39/month on a 2-year plan, NordVPN delivers a combination of speed (892 Mbps on NordLynx), an audited no-logs policy, best-in-class streaming reliability, a native Linux GUI, and threat protection — all on up to 10 simultaneous devices.

Was NordVPN hacked?

Partially — with important context. In 2018, a single rented server in Finland was accessed by an unauthorised party through an insecure remote management interface left open by the third-party hosting provider, not through NordVPN's own systems or code.

A forensic review confirmed the compromised server contained no user data whatsoever — no logs, no browsing history, no credentials. This was because NordVPN's no-logs policy meant there was nothing to find. The breach was disclosed publicly by NordVPN in 2019 — some delay in disclosure is a legitimate criticism, but the absence of any user data exposure is the most important fact. Since then, NordVPN has completed two Deloitte audits, migrated to RAM-only server infrastructure, and terminated the affected hosting relationship. The incident does not change our assessment that NordVPN is a trustworthy, privacy-respecting service.
